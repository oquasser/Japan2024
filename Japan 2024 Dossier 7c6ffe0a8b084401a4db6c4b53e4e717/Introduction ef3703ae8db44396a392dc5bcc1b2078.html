Introduction

I can’t believe this trip is finally here! It has, quite literally, been four years in the making, and it feels surreal that we’re actually taking off on this incredible journey. I’m confident that you all will encounter something on this trip that you’ve never seen before - either in the utter madness that is Tokyo, the ancient charms of Kyoto, the beautiful nature of Nikko, or the amazing food scene in Osaka. More than likely, you’ll take some unique experience away from every place we go.

I’ll do my best to provide as much translation and tour-guiding as possible, but, with such a large group, it seems impossible that we will all be together all of the time. With this eventuality in mind, I’ve collected a bunch of information about each place that we are visiting, as well as some general guidance about traveling in Japan and organized it all into this dossier. The contents of this document were collected to empower you, dear reader, to have the trip that YOU want to have.

The dossier starts with some deep dives into various aspects of Japanese culture that may be confusing or intimidating to the first-time visitor. These are all great topics to read through on the long flight over to Tokyo, where we’ll cover things like emergency information, dining etiquette, how to navigate the rail & subway system of Japan, and many other subjects that are important to know from the very start of the trip. If you read nothing else in this document, please at least read through the Japan 101 - An Introduction section.

After these general Japan-travel-focused topics, the remainder of the dossier is divided in to four sections, one section for each place to which we are traveling: Tokyo, Nikko, Kyoto and Osaka. These sections are in the same order in which we will visit them, so feel free to hold off on reading those sections until we’re in the train heading to that location. For each place, you’ll find sections outlining an overview of the place, including its various districts, various maps, city-specific transit information, notes about our lodging in that location, and a collection of tourist attractions and experiences that you can have in said city. Also, if the location is known for any special culinary dishes, you’ll find a section outlining those dishes and some places to try them. Each section will also conclude with a page called “Ren Suggests ™”, where I’ll highlight any particular points about that location I would recommend you experience.

This will likely come as no surprise, but I definitely took this way too far - there’s so much information in here that I highly doubt anyone will read it all. Where applicable, I’ve included pictures of the various tourist sites so that you can just flip through a section and read about the places which catch your eye. Also, I initially tried to type all of this out on my own, but I quickly resorted to just copy-pasting things from the web, so please excuse any grammar errors or meandering of tone and voice.

Rennan
