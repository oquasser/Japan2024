Tokyo to Nikko 10/21

Shibuya Station to Kinugawa Onsen Station

Summary:

There are two main train options to get us from Tokyo out to Nikko, neither of which leave directly from Shibuya. We’ll need to take local train transport to connecting stations. Between the two options, I think the “Kinugawa” or “Limited Express Spacia Kinugawa” options are the easiest for us. Note that all seats on any of these lines are reservation only - when we book, we should make sure to ask about luggage stowage if anyone has brought large luggage.

  • Tobu Railway From Asakusa Station; there are generally 1-2 limited express trains per hour between Asakusa and Kinugawa Onsen, but Asakusa station is across the city from Shibuya. Also, not to be confused with the “Limited Express Spacia Kinugawa” mentioned below, there is also a “SPACIA X” line operated by Tobu that leaves from Asakusa and connects to Kinugawa Onsen.
  • “Kinugawa” limited express from Shinjuku Station. There are three of these direct trains that leave from Shinjuku everyday and get us directly to Kinugawa Onsen station. Running on the same line as the normal “Kinugawa” service, there is also a new service called “Limited Express Spacia Kinugawa” - both lines are joint operated between JR and Tobu, but the Spacia line uses more modern trains. Trip duration times are about the same. Note that some of these trains don’t go all the way to Kinugawa Onsen, so we want to target the “Kinu”, not the “Kegon”, which terminates at Nikko Station.
