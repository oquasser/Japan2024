Rakushisha Paper Crafts - 洛紙舎

A great souvenir shop with some original, less tacky souvenirs that you might find elsewhere in Japan. In particular, for those looking to purchase nice artwork or copies of famous Japanese paintings, this shop offers a variety of paper prints that are attractive and more sophisticated than those that you will find along Shijo, near Kiyomizu-dera, and other high-traffic tourist areas. There are also authentic Kyoto-made paper fans. Many of the items in this store are difficult to find elsewhere, so if you're interested, it's best to buy them here.

Washi is the traditional paper type whose roots started in Japan. In the 4th century, paper and its manufacturing methods were introduced from the Chinese continent, and by incorporating materials and manufacturing methods local to Japan, washi, a uniquely Japanese paper, was born.

The store sells letter sets and other paper goods, making it the perfect place for those who want to experience Japanese paper culture. It is rare to find a store with as many as 5,000 different kinds of paper!

Being beautiful and lightweight, washi makes for an ideal gift while you are traveling. There are different types of washi you can buy, including kinkarakawa-shi (paper with the texture of tanned leather); shoji-gami(for sliding screens); hosho-shi(writing paper); chochin-gami (for paper lantern); kasa-gami(for umbrellas); ningyo-shi(for dolls); and even senmenshi(for folding fans).

You can easily lose yourself amongst the four floors of displays and shop, which is like an Aladdin’s cave for paper lovers. Paper in all kinds of designs, materials, for home decoration, or as art in its own right.

You can also join the washi paper making workshop which is fun for all ages. You can even learn paper making songs! If you visit, you will be amazed at the variety of Japanese paper.

Getting There

5 minute walk from Karasuma Oike Station on the Kyoto Subway Karasuma Line and Tozai Line
